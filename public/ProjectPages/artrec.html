Contemporary Art Recommendation System

Context and Problem

Studying about careers of contemporary artists, I realized that achieving the status as a “pioneer” artist is correlated more with joining a network of influential dealers and obtaining exhibition opportunities at a few major art institutions than with creativity and artistic skills. These findings inspired me to design a new kind of platform for artists in “peripheral” communities with limited access to renowned art institutions and dealers. The goal was to raise awareness about their work.

Characterizing Users' Artistic Preference

As a part of my senior thesis project for art history at Duke University under the supervision of Prof. Bill Seaman, I built a website that recommends artworks of young contemporary artists based on users’ preferences in terms of extrinsic and intrinsic characteristics of artworks.

Extrinsic characteristics included colors (users could compose a palette of their favorite colors using the interface visualized below), contrast levels, and complexities (characterized using the number of edges detected in a painting).

Intrinsic characteristics included artistic movements each artwork was related to and creative intentions of its artist.



Backend Program of the Recommendation Website

The backend program of the website drew its inspiration from human brains’ visual processing steps, which, according to Eric Kandel’s book, The Age of Insight, involves perceiving colors first and then edges and finally recognizing specific objects. The program abstracted and re-purposed algorithms in computer vision such as Haralick texture analysis, edge detection, and object detection to numerically characterize visible characteristics of artworks. It also used recommendation techniques involving textual tags to capture invisible qualities such as creative purposes and artistic movements.

Conclusion

This project was a great opportunity for me to use an interdisciplinary approach, involving research in art history, computer science, and neuroscience, to address an issue that underrepresented groups of young artists face.

Through this project, I earned the honors of graduating from Duke University with distinction in art history and receiving the Visual and Media Studies Award, which is presented annually to recognize an outstanding senior undergraduate student at Duke who conducted original research in Visual and Media Studies.
